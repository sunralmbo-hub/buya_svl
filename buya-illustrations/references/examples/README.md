# Reference Examples

These folders are visual reference sets for choosing the right mini-comic grammar. Use them to understand character shape, line weight, panel rhythm, emotional staging, and relationship type. Do not copy exact text, layout, props, watermarks, IDs, or a specific original composition.

## 01-crowd-relationship

Use when the article needs crowd logic: ordinary vs special, being submerged, being seen, group texture, or one duck inside many similar ducks.

Reference cues: repeated white duck crowd, pale green background blocks, one ordinary or special duck, dense-but-readable panel composition.

## 02-solo-state

Use when the idea works best as one duck's daily state: tired, stubborn, awkward, pretending to be fine, lazy, quietly proud, or self-contained.

Reference cues: single duck in a simple life scene, one or two panels, minimal text, action-based punchline.

## 03-rescue-relationship

Use when the emotional point is being found, helped, comforted, pulled out, noticed, or approached by another duck.

Reference cues: darker emotional setup, environment or distance first, then a second duck entering as the gentle turn.

## 04-intimate-relationship

Use when the scene needs close interaction: gratitude, hugging, care, companionship, small gifts, mutual support, or soft two-duck body language.

Reference cues: large white space, two ducks close together, small gestures, short handwritten Chinese phrase, gentle emotional landing.

## 05-solo-body-action

Use when the joke depends on one duck's body action or visual transformation: taking off clothes, stretching the neck, awkward movement, sudden reveal, or a physical gag without much text.

Reference cues: two-panel action beat, centered single duck, exaggerated neck/body silhouette, yellow impact marks, minimal or no caption.
